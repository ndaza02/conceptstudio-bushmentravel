Victoria Falls

The Smoke That Thunders - One of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World

Mosi-oa-Tunya

Known locally as "The Smoke That Thunders," Victoria Falls is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most spectacular natural wonders on Earth. With a width of 1,708 meters and height of 108 meters, it forms the largest sheet of falling water in the world.

Urban Wildlife

Unique encounters in Victoria Falls town

Town Wildlife

Victoria Falls town offers unique urban wildlife experiences. Warthogs root around traffic islands, vervet monkeys patrol the streets looking for snacks, and baboons nonchalantly share pavements with visitors.

Elephants frequently wander through town, viewing gardens and swimming pools as fair game. The famous Victoria Falls Hotel cricket club even built a wall to stop elephants strolling across the pitch during matches.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know for your visit

Getting There

By Air

VFA Airport (Victoria Falls) - 20km from town. Regular flights from Harare, Johannesburg, and other regional hubs.

By Road

Well-maintained roads from Harare (880km), Bulawayo (440km), and Kazungula Border (Botswana).

Cross-Border

Easy access from Livingstone, Zambia via Victoria Falls Bridge. KAZA Univisa recommended for multiple crossings.

Accommodation

Luxury

The Victoria Falls Hotel, Elephant Hills Resort, Avani Victoria Falls - historic properties with premium amenities.

Mid-Range

Victoria Falls Safari Lodge, Cresta Sprayview, Lokuthula Lodges - excellent value with good facilities.

Budget

Backpackers, hostels, and guesthouses available in town. Camping options near the falls.
