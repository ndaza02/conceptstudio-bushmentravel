Hwange National Park

Zimbabwe's Premier Wildlife Paradise - Home to Africa's Largest Elephant Herds

Discover More View Wildlife

The Wildlife Kingdom

Hwange National Park ranks among Africa's finest wildlife destinations, boasting the highest diversity of mammals of any national park in the world. With over 108 species and 19 large herbivores, it offers unparalleled wildlife viewing opportunities.

Park Statistics

  • • Area: 14,651 square kilometers
  • • Established: 1928 (as Wankie Game Reserve)
  • • Mammal species: 108 (world's highest diversity)
  • • Large herbivores: 19 species
  • • Large carnivores: 8 species
  • • Bird species: 400+ recorded
  • • Elephant population: 20,000-75,000

Location & Access

Located in northwestern Zimbabwe, bordering Botswana's Chobe National Park. Part of the KAZA (Kavango-Zambezi) Transfrontier Conservation Area - the largest conservation area on Earth.

Main entrance near Main Camp, 200km from Victoria Falls and 280km from Bulawayo. Accessible by road and air.

Wildlife Highlights

Meet the incredible inhabitants of Hwange

The Big Five

  • • Lions: Numerous prides, excellent sightings
  • • Leopards: Elusive but regularly spotted
  • • Elephants: Africa's largest population
  • • Rhinos: Both black and white rhino present
  • • Buffalo: Large herds throughout the park

Predators

  • • Wild Dogs: Endangered, active packs
  • • Cheetahs: Rare but present
  • • Spotted Hyenas: Common and vocal
  • • Jackals: Black-backed and side-striped
  • • Serval & Caracal: Elusive cats

Herbivores

  • • Giraffe: Large populations
  • • Zebra & Wildebeest: Mixed herds
  • • Kudu & Eland: Large antelope species
  • • Impala & Waterbuck: Abundant
  • • Rare Species: Roan, sable, tsessebe

Bird Paradise

Over 400 bird species make Hwange a birdwatcher's heaven

Raptors & Birds of Prey

Hwange hosts around 50 types of raptors, making it one of Africa's premier destinations for birds of prey. Look for:

  • • Martial Eagle, African Hawk Eagle, Bateleur
  • • Various vulture species including White-backed and Lappet-faced
  • • Secretary birds, snake eagles, and goshawks
  • • Owls including Pearl-spotted and Verreaux's Eagle-Owl

Other Notable Species

The park's diverse habitats support an incredible variety of birdlife:

  • • Lilac-breasted Roller, Southern Ground Hornbill
  • • Various bee-eaters, kingfishers, and rollers
  • • Ostriches, kori bustards, and secretary birds
  • • Waterbirds at pans and dams including herons, storks, and egrets

Best Time to Visit

Seasonal guide for optimal wildlife viewing

Dry Season (July-October)

Peak Wildlife Viewing: Best time for game viewing as vegetation thins and animals congregate around waterholes.

Elephant Spectacle: Largest elephant concentrations at pumped waterholes, incredible interactions.

Predator Action: Increased predator activity around water sources.

Weather: Dry, sunny days, cool nights. Minimal rainfall.

Green Season (November-April)

Lush Landscapes: Beautiful green vegetation, dramatic skies.

Bird Watching: Migratory birds arrive, best time for birding.

Baby Animals: Many species give birth during this period.

Considerations: Wildlife more dispersed, some roads may be challenging after heavy rains.

Practical Information

Everything you need for your Hwange safari

Getting There

By Road

200km from Victoria Falls (2.5 hours), 280km from Bulawayo (3.5 hours). 4x4 recommended but not essential in dry season.

By Air

Charter flights available to Main Camp airstrip. Scheduled flights from Victoria Falls possible.

Park Entrances

Main Camp (primary), Ngweshla, Sinamatella. Check opening times and road conditions.

Accommodation

Luxury Lodges

Somalisa, The Hide, Nehimba, Linkwasha - exclusive luxury with excellent guides.

Mid-Range

Hwange Safari Lodge, Ivory Lodge, Khwai - comfortable options with good facilities.

Camping

Main Camp, Sinamatella, and various bush camps. Self-catering options available.

Essential Tips

What to Bring

  • • Neutral-colored clothing (khaki, green, brown)
  • • Warm layers for early morning/night drives
  • • Binoculars and camera equipment
  • • Sun protection and insect repellent
  • • Reusable water bottle

Park Regulations

  • • Stay in vehicles unless at designated areas
  • • No feeding wildlife
  • • Keep to speed limits (40km/h)
  • • Park entry fees: $15-30 per person
  • • Vehicle fees: $10-20 per vehicle
