The Best of Matobo

Get ready for an exciting adventure to the Matobo Hills! Discover a wild place full of history, amazing plants, and animals. Follow rhinos and learn the story of the Bushmen paintings that helped bring these incredible animals back to Matobo National Park. Enjoy stunning views of the land, cool balancing rocks, and special places like the grave of Cecil Rhodes, a European settler. Hear about the Ndebele Kings, early settlers, and the Matabele wars, where 3,000 warriors defeated the Shangani Patrol. Your friendly guide will show you animals and birds and make sure you have a great time. Finish your day with a tasty lunch at our favorite spot in the Matobo Hills, made with local flavors. Wear closed shoes, khakis, a sun hat, sunscreen, and bug spray to stay comfy and enjoy the fun!

Great Zimbabwe

Embark on a captivating journey through the Great Zimbabwe, a city founded in the 9th century and known as Zimbabwe's cultural heartland. Discover the exceptional architectural prowess of the ancestral Shona people as you explore the ruins' freestanding walls and structures made completely of dry stone. Second only to Egypt's pyramids in size, the ruins bear testimony to the might of the state that built the kingdom and the richness of its former culture. Admire the symbolic chevron designs adorning the walls of this ancient African kingdom, paying tribute to the role of fertility. Gain a deeper understanding of Zimbabwe's cultural heritage through a thorough explanation of the ruins, and explore the museum housing original stone carvings of Zimbabwean birds. After a fully immersive tour of these magnificent ruins, often having the entire attraction to yourself, you will be treated to a hearty Zimbabwean lunch. Experience the traditional Mbira music at the cultural village, used as an integral part of life and to communicate with higher spirits. Don't forget to bring along closed-toe walking shoes, a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, and insect repellant for a comfortable adventure.

Khami Ruins Historical Tour

Travel back in time at the Khami Ruins, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that shows the strength of the Torwa Dynasty from the 15th to 17th centuries, after Great Zimbabwe. See the strong stone walls built by the Kalanga people without cement, made to show power, not protection. Visit the King's Residence, where kings lived in big stone areas, while regular people had simple huts. Explore the Sacred Ground, a special place for old rituals, and the Passage Platform, where young girls learned about life and marriage. Check out the Tsoro game board and the Northern and Monolith Platforms. End your day with sunset drinks at the King's Residence, enjoying the beautiful views. Khami Ruins is a great spot to relax with family or friends and uncover an exciting piece of history.

Bulawayo City & Township Tour

Explore the exciting history and culture of Bulawayo, the City of Kings, on this fun tour. Learn about the city's past as the first home of the Ndebele Kings at Bulawayo Dolobho. Visit the Railway Museum to see how Zimbabwe's trains started and check out Cecil Rhodes' personal railway coach from 1897. Admire the beautiful old buildings like the post office, Art Gallery, Exchange Building, City Hall, and Joshua Nkomo statue along the main street. Dive into the lively culture of Makokoba, the oldest township, where our local guide shows you the oldest homes and introduces you to friendly people. Taste delicious local food at nearby spots and stop by the arts and crafts center to see artisans at work. Bring walking shoes, a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, and bug spray to enjoy this amazing trip!
