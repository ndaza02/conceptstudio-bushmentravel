Green Harare

A soft itinerary that moves through gardens, woodlands, and pocket parks – designed to give travellers a day of breathing space in the Sunshine City.

6 min read Woodlands · Highlands

Harare is not just concrete and traffic circles. It is also high canopies, bird calls at dawn, and woodlands that sit surprisingly close to the CBD. "Green Harare" is a day built around shade, slow walks, and picnic blankets.

Harare Gardens · The Central Lawn

Start your morning at Harare Gardens. Slip past the gates with a takeaway coffee and follow the internal paths as they curve between lawns, flower beds and quiet benches. This is where office workers come to read newspapers and where school groups occasionally spill into the open grass.

Look for pockets of dappled light under mature trees. If you have a book, this is where it should be opened. If you have a camera, this is where you start noticing how green frames the city skyline.

Mukuvisi Woodlands · Walking the Trails

A short drive from the centre, Mukuvisi Woodlands offers marked walking and cycling routes through miombo woodland. Antelope and zebra occasionally appear between the trees; birdlife remains an almost constant soundtrack.

Stay on the trails, move quietly, and let your pace be set by birds instead of traffic.

Pocket Parks & Neighbourhood Greens

In suburbs like Highlands and Woodlands, small community parks and sports fields double as evening walking loops. They are where joggers, dog walkers and families intersect at golden hour.
