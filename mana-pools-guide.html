Mana Pools National Park

UNESCO World Heritage Wilderness - Africa's Premier Walking Safari Destination

Discover More View Activities

The Wild Heart of Africa

Mana Pools National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its pristine wilderness and exceptional wildlife viewing. Located on the Zambezi River, this 2,196 square kilometer park offers some of Africa's most authentic safari experiences, particularly famous for walking safaris and canoeing adventures.

Key Features

  • • UNESCO World Heritage Site (1984)
  • • Area: 2,196 square kilometers
  • • Four main pools: Main, Chine, Long, Chisambuk
  • • Part of Middle Zambezi Biosphere Reserve
  • • No permanent lodges - only temporary camps
  • • Famous for walking and canoeing safaris
  • • Remote and pristine wilderness experience

Location & Access

Located in northern Zimbabwe on the southern bank of the Zambezi River, opposite Zambia's Lower Zambezi National Park. The park forms part of a massive transfrontier conservation area.

Access by road from Harare (400km) or by charter flight to Mana Main airstrip. 4x4 essential during rainy season.

Wildlife Highlights

Incredible concentrations and unique behaviors

Large Herbivores

  • • Elephants: Large concentrations, famous for standing on hind legs
  • • Buffalo: Massive herds, excellent predator interactions
  • • Zebra & Wildebeest: Abundant throughout the floodplains
  • • Waterbuck & Eland: Common along riverine areas
  • • Kudu & Impala: Numerous and photogenic

Predators

  • • Lions: Famous prides, excellent sightings
  • • Leopards: Regularly seen, especially along river
  • • Wild Dogs: Active packs, denning sites
  • • Cheetahs: Present but more elusive
  • • Spotted Hyenas: Common and vocal

Unique Behaviors

  • • Standing Elephants: Famous for reaching high branches
  • • Tolerant Wildlife: Animals habituated to walking safaris
  • • River Crossings: Dramatic animal movements
  • • Predator-Prey Dynamics: Excellent predator action
  • • Bird Life: Over 380 species recorded

Safari Activities

Authentic wilderness experiences

Walking Safaris

The Ultimate Experience: Mana Pools is Africa's premier walking safari destination. Expert guides lead you through diverse terrains from riverine forests to open floodplains.

What to Expect: Close encounters with wildlife, tracking experiences, learning about flora and fauna, and the thrill of being on foot in big game country.

Duration: Half-day walks to multi-day walking safaris with overnight camping.

Canoeing Adventures

Zambezi River: Multi-day canoeing safaris along the Zambezi offer unique wildlife viewing from water level. Drift past elephants, buffalo, and numerous bird species.

Experience: Peaceful paddling, excellent photography opportunities, riverside camping, and incredible sunsets over the Zambezi.

Safety: Professional guides, safety briefings, and support boats ensure a secure experience.

Game Drives

Traditional Drives: Morning and afternoon game drives in open vehicles provide excellent wildlife viewing opportunities.

Focus Areas: Riverine areas, floodplains, and around the four main pools where wildlife concentrates.

Photography: Excellent for wildlife photography with abundant subjects and beautiful landscapes.

Fly Camping

Ultimate Wilderness: Mobile fly camping experiences for the adventurous. Sleep under the stars in remote areas of the park.

Experience: Complete immersion in wilderness, night sounds, and exceptional stargazing away from any light pollution.

Comfort: Basic but comfortable camping with hot meals prepared over open fires.

Best Time to Visit

Seasonal guide for optimal experiences

Dry Season (May-October)

Peak Season: Best time for walking safaris and wildlife viewing. Animals concentrate around water sources and riverine areas.

Weather: Dry, sunny days, cool nights. Minimal rainfall, excellent visibility.

Wildlife: Excellent game viewing, predator activity, and elephant concentrations.

Activities: Perfect for walking safaris, canoeing, and all outdoor activities.

Green Season (November-April)

Lush Landscape: Beautiful green vegetation, dramatic skies, and fewer visitors.

Bird Watching: Excellent time for birding with migratory species present.

Challenges: Some areas become inaccessible, walking safaris limited, higher temperatures.

Considerations: Lower rates, more intimate experience, but limited access to some areas.

Practical Information

Essential details for your Mana Pools adventure

Getting There

By Road

400km from Harare (6-7 hours). 4x4 essential, especially during rainy season. Roads can be challenging.

By Air

Charter flights to Mana Main airstrip. Scheduled flights from Harare available seasonally.

Access Points

Main gate at Nyamepi, several other entry points for 4x4 vehicles. Check road conditions before travel.

Accommodation

Luxury Tented Camps

Ruckomechi, Kanga Camp, Chitake Springs - premium tented camps with excellent guides.

Mobile Safaris

Various operators offer mobile walking and canoeing safaris with fly camping.

Self-Catering

Nyamepi campsite and several other public campsites. Basic facilities, bring all supplies.

Essential Tips

What to Bring

  • • Lightweight neutral clothing
  • • Comfortable walking boots
  • • Wide-brimmed hat and sunscreen
  • • Insect repellent (malaria area)
  • • Binoculars and camera
  • • Head torch for camping

Safety Guidelines

  • • Always follow guide instructions
  • • Maintain safe distance from wildlife
  • • No walking without armed guide
  • • Proper malaria prophylaxis essential
  • • Carry sufficient water
  • • Inform someone of your plans

Conservation & Protection

Preserving this pristine wilderness

UNESCO Status

Mana Pools was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984 for its outstanding natural beauty and ecological significance. The park is recognized for its pristine wilderness, exceptional wildlife concentrations, and unique landscape features.

Conservation Challenges

The park faces challenges from poaching, particularly of elephants and rhinos. Conservation efforts focus on anti-poaching patrols, community engagement, and sustainable tourism practices that support both wildlife protection and local communities.
