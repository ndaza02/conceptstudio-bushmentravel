City + Safari in One Trip

How we weave Harare, Bulawayo and Matobo into one coherent journey, instead of separate city breaks and safaris.

8 min read Itinerary Design Â· Zimbabwe

Around the world, more travellers are asking for trips that feel layered: mornings in galleries and markets, evenings watching wildlife at a waterhole. Zimbabwe lends itself naturally to that rhythm. Harare and Bulawayo hold architecture, food and everyday life; Matobo, Great Zimbabwe and Victoria Falls open into rock, history and river.

At Bushmen Travel we think in combinations rather than checklists. This piece sketches a few ways to braid city and safari into one story, drawing on the journals and tours we already run from Harare, Bulawayo and the Matobo Hills.

Three natural entry points

Most Bushmen journeys begin in one of three places:

  • Harare — the capital, galleries, CBD avenues and green suburbs. Our 48 Hours in the Harare CBD journal and other Harare pieces live here.
  • Bulawayo — the City of Kings, railway history, townships and a gateway to Matobo and Khami Ruins.
  • Victoria Falls — a natural anchor for Zambezi time, cross-border add-ons and first-time Zimbabwe trips.

From any of these three you can bend the route in different directions: south into the granite kopjes of Matobo, west to Hwange, or back along the rail and road lines that shaped the country's early urban life.

A one-week Harare & Matobo arc

For guests flying into Harare first, a simple seven-day shape might look like this:

  • Days 1–2 — Harare CBD & Avenues: based on our 48 Hours in the Harare CBD journal, with optional add-ons from the After Dark or Green Harare pieces.
  • Day 3 — Travel south-west: overland towards Bulawayo, with stops chosen for your interests (rail history, small-town markets, landscapes).
  • Days 4–6 — Matobo: combining our Best of Matobo and Matobo Rhino Encounter experiences.
  • Day 7 — Bulawayo city loop: a taste of Bulawayo's CBD and townships before departure, or an extra night if flights allow.

The exact pacing changes with you. Some guests prefer more gallery and cafe time in Harare, others want to reach the granite earlier and walk longer days in the hills.

Bulawayo, Khami Ruins and Victoria Falls

Starting in Bulawayo opens a different rhythm: one that leans into rail lines, stone cities and then the Zambezi.

  • Days 1–2 — Bulawayo CBD & townships: drawing from our Bulawayo 48 Hours, Industrial Heritage and After Dark journals.
  • Day 3 — Khami Ruins: a full day on our Khami Ruins Historical Tour, tracing terraced stone walls and Torwa-era stories.
  • Days 4–5 — Matobo: rhino tracking, cave paintings and World's View.
  • Days 6–7 — Victoria Falls: shifting north for a flexible Victoria Falls Experience with optional Zambezi activities.

Here the story is one of stone and water: Great Zimbabwe and Khami Ruins to the south, the sculpted gorge and spray of the Falls to the north, with Bulawayo and Matobo as connective tissue.

Why small-group pacing matters

Trying to stack four cities and three parks into a week almost always leads to airport lounges instead of street corners, and lodge transfers instead of stories. We design city + safari arcs to feel breathable: two or three bases that you settle into, with enough time for both headline sights and small, ordinary moments.

That often means travelling with a small Bushmen group or as a private party, keeping vehicle sizes modest and avoiding daily check-outs. You begin to recognise the same cafe owner in Bulawayo, the same sunset rock in Matobo, the same stall holder in a Harare market. Those relationships are as much a part of the itinerary as the big views.

Shaping your own version

Every version of this journey starts with a simple question: which parts of Zimbabwe feel most alive to you when you imagine them? Glass-fronted CBD towers at dusk? Township football fields? Rock shelters with Bushmen paintings? Zambezi spray at sunrise?

Once you share that with us — and your rough dates — we sketch two or three possible loops. You might arrive in Harare and leave from Victoria Falls. You might focus entirely on Bulawayo and Matobo with a single long-day add-on. The point is not to tick off names, but to give your week a narrative arc that will still make sense to you a year later.
