Top Destinations

Explore Zimbabwe's most spectacular locations

Victoria Falls

A World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World. The falls support a huge array of activities from white-water rafting to bungee-jumping, along with game drives, boat cruises, and cultural experiences.

The town retains its charm despite commercial success. Wildlife including warthogs, monkeys, baboons, and even elephants wander through town, creating unique urban wildlife encounters.

Discover Victoria Falls →

Hwange National Park

One of Africa's finest national parks, Hwange boasts the highest diversity of mammals of any national park in the world. With over 108 species and 19 large herbivores, it's perfect for your first African wildlife experience.

Home to one of the largest elephant populations on Earth (20,000-75,000), and over 400 bird species including 50 types of raptors. Part of the KAZA Transfrontier Conservation Area.

Explore Hwange →

Mana Pools National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site offering unspoilt natural wilderness like nowhere else in southern Africa. The park features stunning riverine forests, floodplains, and abundant wildlife.

Famous for walking safaris and canoeing adventures along the Zambezi River, with excellent opportunities to see elephants, lions, wild dogs, and diverse birdlife in their natural habitat.

Visit Mana Pools →

Matobo National Park

Home to some of the best examples of San rock paintings in the world, easily accessible and showcasing ancient cave art. The park features dramatic granite formations and diverse wildlife.

A haven for rhinos, leopards, and eagles, with excellent opportunities for hiking, bird watching, and cultural experiences. The spiritual significance of the area adds depth to any visit.

Travel Information

Essential information for your Zimbabwe adventure

When to Visit

Wildlife Viewing

July-October (dry season) for best game viewing around waterholes

Victoria Falls

June-August for optimal balance of water flow and visibility

Bird Watching

Summer months (November-March) for migratory species

Water Sports

August-January for low water levels ideal for rafting

Health & Safety

Medical Facilities

Private clinics and hospitals in Harare, Bulawayo, and Victoria Falls handle emergencies. Comprehensive medical insurance recommended.

Malaria Precautions

Malaria is endemic in many tourist areas. Consult your doctor about prophylaxis before travel.

Drinking Water

Bottled water recommended in towns. Safari lodges typically provide filtered water from boreholes.

General Safety

Zimbabwe is generally safe for tourists. Take normal precautions as you would in any destination.

Visas & Entry

Visa Requirements

Requirements vary by nationality. Check with Zimbabwean embassy for current information.

Visa on Arrival

Available for many nationalities including UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and most EU countries.

KAZA Univisa

$50 visa allows crossing between Zimbabwe and Zambia for 30 days. Available for 65 nationalities.

Entry Requirements

Valid passport, return ticket, and sufficient funds for stay may be required.

Food & Drink

Discover Zimbabwe's culinary delights

Traditional Foods

Sadza

The staple food - a thick maize porridge served with various relishes (nyama). Often rolled into balls and used to scoop up stews.

Game Meats

Excellent quality beef and game meats feature prominently on restaurant menus. Zimbabwean steak is particularly renowned.

Local Specialties

Try traditional dishes like mopane worms, roasted maize, and various wild vegetables and mushrooms when in rural areas.

Beverages

Local Beers

Zimbabwean-brewed lagers like Castle, Zambezi, and Bohlingers are widely available and reasonably priced.

Wines

Imported South African wines are common in bottle stores. Local Zimbabwean wines like Bushman Rock are improving.

Traditional Drinks

Chibuku is a traditional fermented millet beer, popular in rural areas and commercially produced.

Dining Tips

  • • Restaurant prices range from $15-25 for main courses in good establishments
  • • Safari lodges serve set meals - advise dietary requirements when booking
  • • Vegetarian options can be limited in rural areas - plan accordingly
  • • Tap water safety varies - bottled water recommended in most towns

Getting Around

Transportation options for exploring Zimbabwe

Road Travel

4x4 vehicles recommended for national parks and remote areas. Main roads between major cities are generally in good condition.

Car rental available at airports and major cities. International driving license required.

Self-drive offers flexibility but consider guided safaris for wildlife viewing expertise.

Air Travel

Domestic flights connect Harare, Bulawayo, and Victoria Falls. Charter flights available to remote safari areas.

Private air transfers offered by luxury safari operators for convenient access to lodges.

Scheduled flights reduce travel time significantly between distant attractions.
